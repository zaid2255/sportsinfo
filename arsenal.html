W2W4: Liverpool and Arsenal need spark

8 Jan 2018.

With numerous clubs facing the make-or-break part of their seasons, W2W4 highlights the weekend's biggest storylines.

When Liverpool thrashed Arsenal 4-0 at Anfield in August to climb to second place, it looked like Jurgen Klopp's side might finally possess the right blend of talent to mount a serious title challenge. With Emre Can and Georginio Wijnaldum flanking Jordan Henderson in midfield, a front three of Roberto Firmino, Sadio Mane and Mohamed Salah were allowed to run riot, claiming a goal each before Daniel Sturridge wrapped up the result late on. Arsenal, meanwhile, didn't muster a single shot on target. Could this new-look Liverpool possibly have the defensive foundation to summon the requisite staying power at the top?

In a word, no. Arsenal were so shockingly poor on that summer Sunday afternoon that it was almost impossible to gauge Liverpool's true level. A trip to Manchester City in the following game provided a much sterner test and, as they were thumped 5-0, it was clear Klopp still had failed to address the crippling defensive frailties that proved so costly last season, and the season before, and ... you get the picture.

That defeat at the Etihad Stadium started a run of one win in six top-flight matches for Liverpool as City set about breaking the record for consecutive victories. The Reds now trail the leaders by 18 points in fourth place, with Arsenal a point further back in fifth.

On Friday, the battle of the also-rans will commence at the Emirates Stadium. Neither team is in great form; Liverpool have scored a devastating 16 goals in their last four away games but have dropped points at home to Everton and West Brom, while Arsenal have won only once and scored only three times in their last four outings. The main concern for both clubs as they fight for fourth place is that their current travails cannot be explained by that handy excuse of this being a "transition season."

The skipper forgot all this as soon as the questions started pouring in. Speaking about the team’s chances in the semi finals against England on Wednesday, Sarfraz stated that the team’s confidence was on a high and that the recent record in Cardiff favored his side. "We played the last ODI here. We win that time, we chased 300, so definitely our confidence is very high playing in Cardiff," Sarfraz told reporters after Monday s nail-biting triumph.

Klopp may have fashioned a new forward line that can go from 0 to 60mph faster than anyone else in the league, but, recruiting only four new players in the summer, it was obvious the gaps in defence still hadn't been plugged. As for Arsene Wenger, after falling out of the top four for the first time in 20 years, the manager desperately needed to oversee a new sense of direction. Instead, contract standoffs with Alexis Sanchez and Mesut Ozil have been allowed to drift -- the former playing well below his best at the moment -- while Alexandre Lacazette is rarely involved for 90 minutes and the defence, as with Liverpool, still looks worryingly suspect.
